Instructor: Michael Dereszynski

What is this class?

In this dynamic and hands-on course, graduate students will delve into the cutting-edge world of modern infrastructure technologies. Emphasizing real-world applications, ISYS 53333 provides an in-depth exploration of advanced topics in infrastructure including: cloud computing (Infrastructure as a Service and Platform as a Service), identity management, Infrastructure as Code and evolving topics as appropriate each semester. The goal of the course is to equip students with the skills to design, implement, and manage robust and scalable IT infrastructure solutions

Throughout the course, students will:

    Engage in practical exercises to design and implement various infrastructure solutions, focusing on scalability, security, and cost-effectiveness.
    Critically analyze and evaluate different infrastructure technologies, understanding their place and purpose in various business contexts.
    Apply theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios, crafting detailed infrastructure strategies for hypothetical business cases.
    Stay abreast of emerging trends and innovations in infrastructure technology, assessing their potential impact and integration into existing systems.
    Explore the ethical and sustainable dimensions of technology deployment, with a focus on data privacy and environmentally responsible computing practices.
    Gain hands-on experience with the latest tools and platforms in the field, enhancing their practical skill set.
    Work collaboratively on team projects to solve complex infrastructure challenges, fostering skills in communication, teamwork, and innovation.
